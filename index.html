2 · METHODOLOGY — heat sets the balance

HOW TO READ THE CHART (it is the formula drawn, not a picture): the horizontal axis is market heat, cold on the left, hot on the right. Find the white dotted vertical line — that is where the market sits right now. Where the cyan line crosses it = today's % invested target (left axis). The cyan line slopes down because the hotter the market, the less invested you are — buy weakness, sell strength, drawn as a line. The flat gold dashed line = the large-cap share of whatever is invested, and it is set by the DATA (below), not by you.

THE MATH OF THIS PANEL — human words
% INVESTED — a straight line between your two policy endpoints. At heat −1 (maximum washed-out) you hold your max; at +1 (maximum stretched) you hold your floor; in between it is proportional. In words: invested = floor + (max − floor) × how-cold-the-market-is. Those two endpoints are the ONLY dials here, because how much you're willing to hold at the extremes is genuinely your call — everything else the data decides.

LC / SC SPLIT — decided by the data, zero dials. The mechanic: compare the large-cap geiger (SPY) with the small-cap geiger (IWM). The COLDER class gets the BIGGER sleeve, sized by how far apart they are. Formula: LC share = 50% + (IWM geiger − SPY geiger) × 50%, capped between 20% and 80%. If small caps are hotter than large caps, money leans large; if small caps are colder (more washed out), money leans small — the same buy-weakness doctrine, applied BETWEEN classes, no opinion required. The live derivation with today's two geigers is printed above and in panel 6. (The old "LC when hot / SC when cold" sliders are gone — you were right, that was you doing the model's job.)

AGGRESSIVENESS — removed. The curve is fixed linear. If we ever want the model to hold back until things are truly cold, that bend should also come from data (e.g. vol regime), not a hand dial.

PER-POSITION SIZE — each sleeve divided by its position count: that is the unit "one position" that the MOVES panel speaks in.
